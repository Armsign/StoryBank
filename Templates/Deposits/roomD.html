Story Deposit

In Mary Poppins Comes Back, Jane Banks calls herself a hen who has just laid “seven lovely white eggs and I am sitting with my wings over them brooding. Cluck-cluck!”

When PL Travers was a child she pretended to be a hen laying eggs in the garden and wouldn’t come in for dinner, when her mother called!

When creating personalities for a story, you might choose bits and bobs from different people you know, to create a well formed character.

Miss Andrew is the large and often frightening former nanny of Mr Banks. She is a recurring character in the Mary Poppins books who is mean and rude. Nearly everyone is afraid of her, except Mary Poppins!

When creating a story, think about including an unlikeable character. Or think about your characters having unlikeable traits. A dirty, rotten scoundrel will often make a story more interesting for the reader!

George Banks is Mary Poppins's employer. He works at a bank and lives at No. 17 Cherry Tree Lane with his wife and their children. He is rarely present, but is gruffly loving of his wife and children.

The Bird Woman is an elderly woman who sits on the steps of St Paul's Cathedral in London and feeds the birds. She sells bags of crumbs to passers-by for tuppence a bag and is good friends with Mary.

The Bird Woman has conversations with the children about Mary and the nature of shadows. Although she may not look it at first, she proves to be very wise.

Orion appears in Mary Poppins in Cherry Tree Lane when he comes down to earth from the stars above. He is a friend of Mary’s.

In Greek mythology, he was a giant huntsman whom Zeus, King of the Gods, placed among the stars as the constellation of Orion.
